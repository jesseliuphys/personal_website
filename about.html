About



CV

I am an incoming Assistant Professor of Physics at New York University arriving in Spring 2025. As a particle physicist, I collaborate with scientists on the ATLAS Experiment at CERN in Geneva, Switzerland.

My research currently focuses on using photon collisions in ATLAS for measuring the tau-lepton magnetic moment to probe new physics, and building the brand new Inner Tracker (ITk) strips detector. I have also spent several years exploring dark matter searches at the Large Hadron Collider and small-scale axion experiments.

Originally from London, UK, I completed my BA at the University of Oxford, MSc at the Perimeter Institute and University of Waterloo, before returning to Oxford for my PhD. I then undertook postdoctoral research as a Grainger Fellow at the University of Chicago, and Junior Research Fellow at Trinity College, University of Cambridge.

I am the first in my family to study at university. I am invested in mentoring, especially those from similar backgrounds, to help navigate the well-documented challenges faced by this community and foster belonging, while attracting newcomers to science via outreach. I have grapheme–colour synaesthesia.
