About



Short CV

I am a Grainger Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Chicago. I am also a Junior Research Fellow at Trinity College, University of Cambridge, where I am on leave until 2021–25.

My research in particle physics involves devising new measurements and searches for particles and forces at extreme laboratory regimes. My cross-cutting work spans hardware to phenomenology and I collaborate in the ATLAS Experiment at CERN's Large Hadron Collider.

I am the first in my family to attend university. I am part of the First-Generation, Low-Income, Immigrant (FLI) network, where I am always happy to offer advice and foster an inclusive community. There is a place for you in university, science and research.

Originally from London, England, I moved to the University of Oxford and graduated with a BA in 2014. I spent a year at the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics supervised by Natalia Toro and received my MSc from the University of Waterloo. I then returned to Oxford to complete my PhD with a two-year stay at CERN, supervised by Alan Barr and John March-Russell.

I enjoy doing science outreach, travelling, classical music and graphic design. As an undergraduate, I was a resident student advisor for two years to US visiting students on the Williams–Exeter Programme at Oxford. I also designed publicity and played the violin for numerous university ensembles. I have grapheme–colour synaesthesia.
